Role Overview

Venus owns the day-to-day execution of Dollar Commerce's social media presence across Instagram, X (Twitter), and Substack. Her focus is admin and volume — running consistent daily output, engagement, and community building.

The core mission: grow the DC community across three platforms by posting daily market updates on DC Index performance and top movers in the e-commerce space, amplifying new DC articles on publish, and driving outbound engagement that builds a high-quality following in our target demographic.

She is not responsible for design work or Instagram feed posts anymore. Design assets and templates will be provided; her job is to populate, publish to Stories / X / Substack, engage, and grow.

Daily Market Update Post

Sample format for the daily DC Index update. Post as a thread on X, as a Note on Substack. On Instagram, share as a Story (no feed post).

DC Index — Daily Update
DC Index (weighted) +2.3% today.
DC Software+1.0%
DC Brands-4.0%
DC Marketplaces+1.10%
Biggest mover (up): Allbirds (BIRD) +582% on news of its pivot to AI infrastructure, two weeks after selling its shoe business for $39M.
Biggest mover (down): Warby Parker (WRBY) -8% after missing Q1 revenue estimates and guiding lower for the year.
Data as of close · April 17, 2026

Post Structure Rules

  • Open with the weighted DC Index move (always lead with this)
  • List all three sub-indexes with colored % (green up, red down)
  • One biggest winner, one biggest loser — always with the why
  • "Why" must reference actual news or earnings, never speculation
  • Close with data timestamp — never post without it
  • If markets closed flat (<0.3% move), lead with the most interesting individual mover instead

Core Responsibilities

Six recurring deliverables. The daily market update is non-negotiable — everything else flexes around it.

Daily · Every market day

DC Index Market Update (X & Substack)

Pull the daily performance data from the DC Index dashboard after market close. Post the standardized market update (see sample above) as a thread on X and a Note on Substack. On Instagram, share as a Story only.

  • Pull data from DC Index after market close
  • Identify top 1 gainer and top 1 loser with news hook
  • Research the "why" — check earnings releases, press releases, news
  • Post as X thread + Substack Note + IG Story by 5:30pm ET
Daily · 2-3 Stories

Instagram Stories

Venus does not post to the Instagram feed. Her IG output is Stories only — 2-3 per day. This keeps the account active in followers' trays and drives traffic to DC content without adding to the grid.

  • 1 daily Story with a link to the DC Daily page (non-negotiable)
  • 1-2 additional Stories sharing links to previous DC articles (from the archive)
  • Mix in polls, questions, or reshared content occasionally to drive engagement signals
  • Use the DC branded Story template (provided)
  • Tag relevant brands, people, or publications when sharing article links
On publish · New articles

New Article Launch (X)

Any time DC publishes a new article, Venus posts a short snippet on X with an excerpt and a link back to the full piece. This is the primary social amplifier for new content.

  • Pull a punchy 1-2 line excerpt from the article (hook, stat, or strong line)
  • Add context in her own words — not just a repost of the headline
  • Include link to the full article
  • Tag relevant people/brands mentioned in the article
  • Post within 1 hour of article going live
  • Also share the article link as an Instagram Story that day
Daily

Engagement & Outbound

Active engagement across all three platforms to drive follower growth and algorithmic reach. Quality over quantity — no engagement pods, no hustle-grind accounts.

  • Comment on 10-15 relevant Instagram accounts per day
  • Reply to 15-20 conversations on X per day
  • Restack 1-2 relevant Substack posts per day (with commentary)
  • Comment on other Substack writers' Notes daily
  • Follow 5-10 quality accounts per day (see approved accounts list)
  • Reply to every comment / DM on our content within 1 hour
Daily

News Monitoring & Rapid Response

Stay on top of e-commerce, DTC, and commerce platform news throughout the day. Breaking stories should be reacted to on X within a few hours.

  • Morning scan of approved news sources (see source list)
  • Flag breaking stories to Andrew for approval if sensitive
  • Draft and publish rapid-response X posts within the day
  • Monitor X for breaking news from founders and journalists
Weekly

Reporting & Admin

Weekly snapshot of growth, engagement, and top-performing content across all three platforms.

  • Friday EOD report: follower growth, top post, engagement rate per platform
  • Flag content that overperformed for replication
  • Maintain running list of engaged-with accounts (avoid repeat spam)
  • Log which archive articles are being reshared to Stories (avoid repetition)

Guardrails

Hard rules. When in doubt, flag to Andrew before posting.

  • Never post speculative "why" for market moves — always tie to actual news/earnings
  • Never engage with hustle-grind accounts ("make $10K/month") or engagement pods
  • Never post crime content, partisan politics, or culture-war topics
  • Never take political sides in replies — keep all engagement brand-safe
  • Always credit data sources (DC Index, WHOOP, Bloomberg, etc.)
  • Always flag sensitive news stories to Andrew before posting
  • Always check DC article calendar before planning the day — new articles take priority
  • Never share internal metrics, revenue, or private founder conversations
