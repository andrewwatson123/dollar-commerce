Brand Identity & Persona

▼

Target Audience

22-30 year old city professional

Think SW London or NYC. Works in finance, tech, or consulting. Active lifestyle — gym before work, sauna on weekends. Reads Bloomberg on the commute, listens to podcasts on DTC brands. Higher income bracket, style-conscious but not flashy. Cares about building something, not just consuming.

Interests

Entrepreneurship Finance & Markets E-Commerce / DTC Sport Longevity & Health Wellness / Biohacking

Voice & Tone

Smart, not pretentious. Data-driven with a pulse on culture. We can be funny — not corporate. Think "your well-read mate who works in VC" energy. Short, punchy captions. Strong opinions backed by data.

Visual Identity

Font: PT Serif for post graphics
Colors: Dark backgrounds, cream text, red accents
Style: Clean, minimal, high-contrast. Numbered carousels. Bold data callouts. No stock photo energy.

Content Pillars

▼

Seven rotating pillars. DC Articles take priority when published (2-3/week). Remaining slots filled from the other six pillars based on what's trending that day.

Priority

DC Articles

When we publish a new article on Dollar Commerce, it gets featured that day. Carousel or single-image with key takeaway + link in bio.

Frequency: 2-3x/week when published
Format: Carousel, article feature graphic
Timeliness: Same day as publish
News

E-Commerce, Platforms & Brand News

Breaking stories about DTC brands, marketplaces, funding rounds, launches, failures — plus platform-level news: Shopify updates, Meta/Google ad platform changes, TikTok Shop moves, new ad software features, algorithm shifts. Anything that affects how commerce gets done online.

Frequency: 3-4x/week
Format: Numbered news carousel, single stat card
Timeliness: Within 24 hours of breaking
Includes: Shopify, Meta Ads, Google Ads, TikTok, Amazon, Klaviyo, ad tech updates
Culture

Startup & Founder Culture

Founder stories, startup lessons, funding milestones, "what I learned" angles. Relatable to our demo who are building or want to build.

Frequency: 2-3x/week
Format: Carousel, quote card, video clip
Timeliness: Evergreen OK, trending preferred
Health & Science

Longevity, Wellness & Biotech

Sauna, ice bath, sleep science, biohacking, WHOOP data, fitness — plus new clinical studies, biotech fundraising rounds, and breakthrough science. Dig deeper than mainstream news: preprint servers, niche biotech journals, clinical trial registries. Our demo lives this lifestyle and wants to be ahead of the curve.

Frequency: 2-3x/week
Format: Data card, stat graphic, study breakdown carousel, video clip
Timeliness: New studies & biotech funding ASAP, practical tips evergreen
Sources: Not just public news — dig into bioRxiv, ClinicalTrials.gov, Fierce Biotech, STAT News, Endpoints News, Crunchbase for biotech rounds
Sport

Sport & Culture

Where sport meets business. NIL deals, athlete brands, major event data (like Masters WHOOP), crossover stories.

Frequency: 1-2x/week
Format: Data carousel, video clip, hot take
Timeliness: Event-driven, ride the wave
Markets

Finance & Markets

DC Index movers, interesting stock stories, macro trends affecting commerce. Make finance visual and accessible.

Frequency: 2x/week
Format: Data card, chart graphic, carousel
Timeliness: Same day for market moves
Local

London & NYC

Localized business news for our two core cities. New policy affecting young professionals or startups, business openings/closings, local tech scene moves, tax or regulation changes impacting UK/US founders. If it matters to a 26-year-old building something in Fulham or the West Village, it belongs here.

Frequency: 1-2x/week
Format: News carousel, single card, hot take
Timeliness: Within 24-48hrs of announcement
Scope: Business & policy only — no crime, no partisan politics

Post Formats & Templates

▼

Mix formats to keep the grid visually interesting. Aim for no two adjacent posts in the same format.

📄

Numbered Carousel

PT Serif headers. Red number at top. 3-8 slides. Our signature format for news and breakdowns.

📈

Data / Stat Card

Single bold stat with context. Dark bg, colored accents. Great for WHOOP-style data, market moves.

📰

Article Feature

Promote DC articles. Hero image + headline + "Link in bio". Can be single or carousel.

💬

Quote / Hot Take

Bold opinion on clean background. PT Serif quote, minimal design. Drives comments.

🌴

Lifestyle Image

Full-bleed photo. Product shots, city life, wellness aesthetic. Breaks up the data-heavy posts.

🎬

Video Clip

15-60s clips sourced from YouTube, podcasts, interviews. Add captions. Relevant founder/health content.

Grid Aesthetic Goal

When someone lands on the profile, the grid should alternate between: dark data graphics, lifestyle photos, and carousel covers. No two visually identical posts side by side. The vibe is "this person knows things" not "this is a content mill."

Engagement Playbook

▼

💬 Post Engagement (Critical)

  • Reply to every comment within 30-60 min of posting
  • Like all comments on our posts
  • Pin the best comment or conversation starter
  • Add a question in captions to drive replies
  • Use Stories to tease the post and drive to grid

🚀 Outbound Daily

  • Comment thoughtfully on 10-15 relevant accounts
  • Follow 5-10 quality accounts in our demo
  • Engage 15 min BEFORE posting (warms the algo)
  • Engage 15 min AFTER posting (signals activity)
  • Share relevant content to Stories with our take

🎯 Who to Engage

  • Founders building real companies
  • Quality content creators in our pillars
  • People in our demographic (city, active, professional)
  • Relevant journalists and editors
  • Brands we cover or admire

🚫 Who NOT to Engage

  • "Learn how to make $10K/month doing ___" accounts
  • Generic motivational quote pages
  • Dropshipping/get-rich-quick gurus
  • Bots, pod groups, or engagement-farming accounts
  • Anything that wouldn't pass the "would I follow this IRL?" test

📌 Tagging & Mentions

  • Tag brands/people discussed in posts (they often reshare)
  • Mention data sources (WHOOP, Pitchbook, etc.)
  • Use location tags for London/NYC when relevant
  • Tag DC in all cross-posted content

🕑 Stories Strategy

  • Post 3-5 Stories daily (keeps us in the tray)
  • Mix: reshare grid posts, polls, behind-the-scenes, quick takes
  • Use polls/questions for engagement signals
  • Highlight trending topics or breaking news fast

Do's and Don'ts

▼

✅ Do

  • Use data and stats in every carousel — our audience loves numbers
  • Have a strong opinion in captions. Don't be neutral wallpaper
  • Mix formats constantly: carousel, single image, video, quote
  • Post natively (not from schedulers) when possible
  • Engage genuinely — thoughtful comments, not "great post!"
  • Credit sources and tag people/brands discussed
  • Use PT Serif consistently in post graphics
  • Keep captions punchy. Lead with the hook, not context
  • Feature DC articles prominently when published
  • Reference the DC Index when discussing market moves
  • Stay on top of breaking news — first to post wins
  • Use Stories daily even on lower-effort days
  • Dig deeper for health/biotech — preprints, clinical trial registries, niche journals beat mainstream
  • Include platform-level news (Shopify, Meta Ads, etc.) alongside brand stories
  • Post localized London/NYC business news that our demo actually cares about
  • Keep policy posts factual and business-focused, never partisan

❌ Don't

  • Follow or engage with "make money online" / hustle grind accounts
  • Post stale news (>24hrs for ecom, >48hrs for everything else)
  • Use generic stock photos — every image should feel intentional
  • Write corporate copy. No "We're excited to announce..."
  • Post without a caption hook in the first line
  • Ignore comments — reply to everything within the first hour
  • Use too many hashtags (3-5 max, relevant ones only)
  • Copy competitors' exact angles — add our own take
  • Post identical content across all platforms with no adaptation
  • Engage with bots or join engagement pods
  • Sacrifice quality for the 2x/day cadence — 1 great post beats 2 mid ones
  • Forget to check DC for new articles before planning the day
  • Post crime news, violent stories, or anything sensationalist
  • Take political sides or post contentious partisan content (unless it's a DC article Andrew has written)
  • Post opinions on divisive social/political topics — we're a business brand, stay in lane

🚨 Content Guardrails

These are hard rules, not guidelines:

  • NO crime content. Never post about crime, violence, or public safety incidents.
  • NO partisan politics. Unless it's a DC article Andrew has personally written, we don't take political sides. Period.
  • NO culture war topics. If Twitter is fighting about it, we're not weighing in.
  • YES to policy that affects business. Tax changes, regulation, startup-friendly legislation — frame it as "what this means for founders" not "which party did what."
  • When in doubt, skip it. If you're unsure whether something is too political or contentious, don't post it. Flag it for Andrew instead.

The Litmus Test

Before posting anything, ask: "Would a 26-year-old working in finance in Fulham or the West Village stop scrolling for this?" If not, rethink it.
